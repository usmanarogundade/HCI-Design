Social Insurance Number Application Guide

Type: General

Brief: The Social Insurance Number (SIN) is a 9 digit number that you need to work in Canada or to have access to government programs and benefits. A SIN is issued to one person only and it cannot legally be used by anyone else

What to bring:

Temporary residents (Immigrants) must provide one of the following documents as an identity document:

  • work permit 
  • study permit
  • visitor record
  • diplomatic identity card and a work authorization note/letter issued by the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development.

If the name on your primary proof of identity document is different from the name you currently use. You will need a supporting proof-of-identity document.

Where to Apply: 

 Applying at a Service Canada office nearest you (Offices:

  1.  Bring all the necessary documents to the nearest Service Canada Centre. 
  2.  If everything is in order, you will get a SIN during your visit.

Normally you must apply for a SIN in person or have someone else apply for you in person. However:  if you live in a remote area with no Service Canada Centre within 100 km, you are eligible to apply by mail. To confirm this is the case, you can use your postal code to check your eligibility on the Service Canada website or call Service Canada at 1-800-206-7218 (select option 3).

If you are unable to apply in person or to have someone else apply for you in person, you may be eligible to apply by mail. You must call Service Canada at 1-800-206-7218 (select option 3) in order to find out if you can apply by mail.

Health Card Application Guide

Type: Health

Brief: The Manitoba Health card allows you to see a doctor, get a prescription, visit a hospital emergency room or use most other health services. You should register for health coverage as soon as you arrive.

What to bring: 

  • Completed Manitoba Health Registration Form 
  • Permanent Resident Confirmation card (copy of both sides) or your Record of Landing (IMM1000).
  • Previous province’s/territory’s health card (if applicable).
  • Supporting documents (as of 2019):
    1. Signed mortgage agreement
    2. Signed long term rental or lease agreement (6 months or more)
    3. Employment confirmation (dated letter from employer on company letterhead)
    4. Notarized letter from the homeowner or leaseholder stating (1) names Of the applicants for Manitoba health
    5. coverage living with them in residence, and (2) length of stay
    6. Notarized letter regarding the applicant's residence in Manitoba from a sponsorship agreement holder group

          OR, any two of these documents, subject to documents being acceptable:

  1. utility bill — telephone, cable/satellite, TV, gas, water/sewer (not Older than 2 months)
  2. Insurance policy (home or tenant)
  3. property bill (current year)
  4. Valid Manitoba Driver's Licence
  5. Valid Manitoba Motor Vehicle registration
  6. Confirmation of attendance from a school, college or university (not older than two months)
  7. Revenue Canada Income Tax Assessment (current or previous year) showing Manitoba as residence
  8. Social Assistance Benefit Confirmation
  9. Employment and Income Assistance Statement of Benefits
  10. Child Tax Benefit statement
  11. Canadian Pension statement of contribution or statement of benefits
  12. Old Age Security Statement

If you cannot provide any of the above documents, provide all documents that you have that may provide proof of residency in Manitoba for MHAL'S review.

Where to Apply: 

Manitoba Health, 

300 Carlton St.,

Winnipeg MB.
